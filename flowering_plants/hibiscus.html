Hibiscus

The hibiscus plant, belonging to the genus Hibiscus, is a stunning flowering shrub or small tree known for its large, showy blooms and lush foliage. Typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters (3 to 10 feet), hibiscus plants can vary in size and shape, depending on the species and growing conditions. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and often lobed or serrated, providing an attractive backdrop for the vibrant flowers. Hibiscus flowers are characterized by their trumpet-shaped structure, with five or more petals that can come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, orange, yellow, and white, often featuring contrasting centers or markings. These flowers can be quite large, measuring up to 30 centimeters (12 inches) in diameter, and bloom in succession, creating a stunning display throughout the growing season. Hibiscus thrives in warm, sunny climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it popular in gardens, landscapes, and as ornamental houseplants.
